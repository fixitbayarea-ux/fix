GE Appliance Repair

GE Appliances operates four distinct product tiers — standard GE, GE Profile, GE Cafe, and Monogram — and each tier uses different internal components, control logic, and build quality. A diagnostic approach that works on a standard GE gas range will not necessarily apply to a Cafe induction cooktop, even though both carry the GE name. The control boards are different, the sensor configurations are different, and the error code systems are different. This tiered engineering is what makes GE one of the more complex brands to repair correctly.

In our Bay Area service area, we see the full GE spectrum: standard GE units in apartment complexes and rental properties, Profile and Cafe models in renovated kitchens throughout San Francisco, and Monogram built-ins in high-end Peninsula homes. FixitBay technicians service all four GE tiers daily and understand the engineering differences between them — from the basic mechanical controls on entry-level units to the WiFi-connected smart boards on Cafe and Monogram models.

Common Problems We Fix

  • GE refrigerator main control board failure — erratic temperature swings, blinking display, and intermittent cooling on French door models
  • GE Profile dishwasher leaves food on the top rack — spray arm hub has worn down, reducing water pressure to the upper assembly
  • GE Cafe range igniter clicks but gas never flows — gas safety valve solenoid coil has failed and won't open the gas line
  • GE washer stuck on sensing cycle — lid lock assembly has failed or the pressure switch hose has disconnected from the tub
  • GE gas dryer runs but produces no heat — one or both gas valve solenoid coils have failed, a common wear item
  • GE refrigerator ice dispenser jammed — auger motor gear has stripped or ice has clumped together in the chute from a door seal gap
  • GE oven F97 error code — door lock motor unable to engage, preventing the oven from entering bake or self-clean mode
  • GE Profile induction cooktop flashing E — cookware detection sensor misaligned or cracked glass interfering with the magnetic field

What to Check Before You Call

  • If your GE fridge display is blinking, try holding the Temperature Up button for 5 seconds to exit diagnostic mode
  • Check if the GE dishwasher float switch at the bottom of the tub is stuck in the raised position — this prevents the unit from filling
  • For a GE washer stuck on sensing, confirm the lid closes completely and the magnetic latch audibly clicks
  • Verify your GE oven clock is set — certain GE models will not heat if the clock display is flashing
  • Note the first 3 characters of your GE model number (PFE, CTS, JBS, etc.) — this tells us the exact tier and helps us prepare the right parts

FAQ

Do you repair all GE tiers including Profile, Cafe, and Monogram?

Yes. Profile and Cafe units make up a large portion of our GE repair calls. They use different control boards and sensor arrays than standard GE, and Monogram uses commercial-grade compressors and panel-ready cabinetry. We service all four tiers.

My GE French door fridge control board keeps failing. Is this a known issue?

Yes. Certain GE French door models manufactured between 2014 and 2019 have documented main control board failures. When available, we install an updated revision board that addresses the root cause of the original design flaw.

How much does a GE range igniter replacement cost?

A GE oven or range igniter replacement typically costs $180 to $280 including the diagnostic credit, the part, and labor. It is one of the most common and straightforward GE repairs we perform.

My GE dishwasher leaves residue on dishes. Is it broken?

Not necessarily broken, but something is underperforming. The most common causes are a worn spray arm hub that reduces water pressure, a clogged chopper blade, or water temperature below 120°F at the inlet. We test all three during the diagnostic visit.

Are GE Monogram appliances more expensive to repair?

Somewhat. Monogram units use commercial-grade components and panel-ready installation that requires more labor time to access. Parts also cost more than standard GE. However, given Monogram replacement costs of $5,000 to $15,000, repair almost always makes financial sense.
