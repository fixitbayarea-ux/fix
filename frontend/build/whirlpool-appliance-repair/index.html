Common Problems We Fix

  • Whirlpool refrigerator not making ice — water inlet valve frozen shut or ice maker ejector module motor has failed
  • Whirlpool washer F5E2 error — door lock mechanism is jammed or the lid switch has broken internally
  • Whirlpool dryer produces no heat — thermal fuse has blown, almost always caused by restricted exhaust vent airflow
  • Whirlpool dishwasher clean light blinking 7 times — internal heating element or high-limit thermostat has failed
  • Whirlpool washer won't drain — drain pump clogged with a sock, coin, or small garment that slipped past the basket
  • Whirlpool gas oven won't ignite — hot surface igniter is cracked or drawing below the 3.0 amps needed to open the gas valve
  • Whirlpool dryer makes a loud squealing noise — drum support rollers are worn flat or the drive belt is fraying
  • Whirlpool refrigerator leaking water onto the floor — defrost drain tube behind the back panel is frozen with ice buildup

What to Check Before You Call

  • Check if your Whirlpool dishwasher door latches fully — the blinking clean-light code often means the latch isn't engaging the strike plate
  • For a Whirlpool washer that won't drain, check the coin trap behind the lower-left access panel on front-load models
  • If your Whirlpool dryer has no heat, check both legs of the 240V circuit breaker — one tripped leg removes heat but leaves the drum spinning
  • Try resetting your Whirlpool appliance by unplugging it for 60 seconds and plugging it back in
  • Listen for clicking, humming, or grinding sounds and note when they happen — this tells us which component to bring

FAQ

Are Whirlpool repairs expensive compared to other brands?

Whirlpool repairs are among the most affordable because parts are widely available and competitively priced. Most repairs run $150 to $350 including the $60 diagnostic credit, replacement part, and labor.

My Whirlpool washer shakes across the floor during spin. What causes that?

Top-load washers shake when suspension rods stretch or the tub bearing wears out. Front-loaders shake from deteriorated shock absorbers. Both are fast repairs that resolve the problem permanently.

How often should I clean my dryer exhaust vent?

At least once per year — more often if the vent run exceeds 15 feet or has multiple elbows. A clogged vent is the number one cause of Whirlpool dryer no-heat calls and also a significant fire hazard.

My Whirlpool ice maker stopped working. Could it be the water filter?

Possibly. A filter past its 180-day life can restrict water flow enough to starve the ice maker. We check the filter, the inlet valve, and the ice maker module itself to isolate the specific cause.

How long do Whirlpool appliances usually last?

Whirlpool refrigerators typically last 12 to 18 years, washers 10 to 14 years, and dryers 12 to 16 years. Their straightforward mechanical design means fewer complex failures and longer service life than technology-heavy competitors.

Should I repair or replace my Whirlpool appliance?

Whirlpool appliances are worth repairing if under 10–12 years old and the repair is under 50% of replacement cost. Most Whirlpool refrigerators and washers last 12–15 years with proper maintenance. Our technician gives an honest recommendation after the $60 diagnostic — no pressure either way.
