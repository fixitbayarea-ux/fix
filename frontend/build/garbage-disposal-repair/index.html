Common Garbage Disposal Problems We Fix

  • Disposal jammed or stuck — foreign objects, fibrous foods, or dull blades prevent the grinding plate from turning. We clear jams safely and inspect for blade wear.
  • Won't turn on (no power) — faulty reset switch, tripped breaker, or wiring issue. We diagnose electrical problems and replace defective components.
  • Humming but not spinning — the motor energizes but the flywheel is stuck. Often caused by a small object wedged between the impeller and shredder ring.
  • Leaking from bottom or connections — worn mounting gasket, cracked housing, or loose drain connections cause water to pool under the sink.
  • Slow draining or backing up — clogged drain line, blocked dishwasher connection, or partially jammed grinding chamber restricts water flow.
  • Bad odors — food buildup and bacterial growth inside the grinding chamber. We clean the unit and advise on prevention.

When to Repair vs. Replace Your Garbage Disposal

Repair: Jammed blades or humming — most jams are cleared in 15–30 minutes for $80–$150. The motor is fine.

Repair: Leaking from the flange or drain connection — a worn gasket or loose fitting costs $90–$140 to fix.

Replace: Motor burned out or unit won't turn on — motor replacement often costs as much as a new disposal ($150–$300 installed).

Replace: Unit is 10+ years old with chronic jams — a new 3/4 HP disposal runs $180–$300 installed and handles food waste far better.
