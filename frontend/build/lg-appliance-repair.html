LG Appliance Repair

LG staked its appliance reputation on two technologies: the linear compressor in refrigerators and the direct-drive motor in washers. Both eliminated major failure points found in conventional designs — the linear compressor removed the connecting rod and piston found in reciprocating compressors, while the direct-drive motor removed the belt and pulley system entirely. In theory, fewer moving parts means fewer failures. In practice, LG's linear compressors developed their own reliability problems significant enough that LG extended compressor warranty coverage to 10 years on affected models.

For Bay Area homeowners, this means two things: your LG refrigerator compressor might be covered under an extended warranty you didn't know existed, and your LG repair technician needs to know the difference between a $200 fan motor swap and a warranty-eligible compressor replacement. Common Problems We Fix

  • LG refrigerator warm with compressor clicking — linear compressor has failed internally, a known issue on certain model years
  • LG washer OE drain error — pump filter packed with coins, bobby pins, or fabric scraps preventing impeller rotation
  • LG dryer showing d80 or d90 Flow Sense warning — exhaust vent lint buildup restricting airflow below the safe operating threshold
  • LG dishwasher AE error code — water leak detected at the sump gasket or main wash motor seal
  • LG refrigerator ice maker stops producing — water inlet valve stuck closed or ice maker ejector motor burned out from overwork
  • LG washer shaking hard on spin cycle — one or more shock absorbers have lost damping fluid after years of use
  • LG oven won't preheat — bake or broil element has open-circuited at the hidden rear terminal behind the back panel
  • LG refrigerator compressor runs nonstop — low refrigerant charge from a micro-leak in the sealed system, often at a brazed joint

What to Check Before You Call

  • If your LG fridge is warm, check the condenser coils behind the kick plate at the bottom — heavy dust buildup causes overheating
  • For LG washer OE error, find the drain filter access panel on the lower-front of the machine, open it, and clean the filter
  • Check your LG dryer exhaust vent for lint accumulation — the d80 code means airflow is critically restricted
  • Try a hard reset on your LG dishwasher by holding the Power button for 5 seconds until the display clears
  • Note any error codes and when they appear — during startup, mid-cycle, or at the end — this pattern speeds up diagnosis significantly

FAQ

Is the LG linear compressor problem really that common?

Common enough that LG extended warranty coverage on the compressor to 10 years on many models. If your refrigerator is within that window, the compressor part itself may be covered. We diagnose the issue first and then help you determine warranty eligibility.

My LG front-load washer smells musty even after cleaning cycles. Why?

Front-load washers trap moisture in the door boot seal folds and detergent dispenser housing. We deep-clean both areas, treat with an antimicrobial solution, and show you a 30-second routine after each wash to prevent recurrence.

How long does a typical LG repair take?

Most LG repairs finish in 45 to 90 minutes. Linear compressor replacements are the exception — they take 2 to 3 hours because the sealed system requires refrigerant recovery, copper brazing, and precise recharge.

What does the LG dryer d80 code mean if the lint filter is already clean?

The d80 code measures total exhaust system restriction, not just the lint filter. The transition duct behind the dryer, the wall duct, and the exterior vent flap can all accumulate lint over time. We test airflow at each point to find the blockage.

Can you repair the LG InstaView door panel?

Yes. InstaView panel replacements and LED backlight repairs are a regular call for us. We source the correct panel for your model and recalibrate the knock-to-view sensor after installation.
