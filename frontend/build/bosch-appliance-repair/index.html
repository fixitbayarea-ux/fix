Bosch Appliance Repair

Bosch built its reputation on one obsession: quiet. Their dishwashers routinely measure under 44 decibels — quieter than a library — and that engineering philosophy extends to their entire appliance line. In the Bay Area, Bosch dishwashers are the single most common European appliance we encounter, followed by their heat-pump dryers and counter-depth refrigerators. The reason Bosch repairs differ from standard American brands is the underlying technology: condensation drying instead of heated drying, inverter motors instead of belt-driven motors, and heat-pump compression instead of resistance heating.

These engineering choices make Bosch appliances more energy-efficient and longer-lasting, but they also mean that a generic repair approach often leads to misdiagnosis. A Bosch dishwasher that "doesn't dry" isn't missing a heating element — it uses a fundamentally different drying method that relies on stainless-steel interior walls and temperature differentials. FixitBay LLC technicians work on Bosch units daily across San Francisco, the Peninsula, and Marin County, and we understand these systems at the component level.

Common Problems We Fix

  • Bosch dishwasher E24 error — drain pump obstructed by food debris or a failed check valve preventing water from leaving the sump
  • Bosch dishwasher not drying dishes — condensation drying system underperforming due to blocked vent or empty rinse-aid dispenser
  • Bosch washer E18 code — drain filter clogged with lint and small objects, or pump impeller jammed and unable to rotate
  • Bosch heat-pump dryer shutting off mid-cycle — condenser fins matted with lint, reducing heat exchange below the operating threshold
  • Bosch refrigerator not reaching set temperature — evaporator fan motor seized or main control board relay stuck in off position
  • Bosch oven not heating past 300°F — bake element open-circuited at the terminal connection behind the rear panel
  • Bosch induction cooktop displaying E3 — cracked glass surface disrupting the magnetic field sensor underneath the cooking zone
  • Bosch dishwasher E15 alarm — water has reached the base pan and tripped the float-style leak sensor, often from a loose hose clamp

What to Check Before You Call

  • Check if your Bosch dishwasher filter basket is blocked — remove it from the sump and rinse under running water
  • Restart your Bosch washer by unplugging it for 60 seconds, then plugging back in to clear temporary error codes
  • If you have a ventless dryer, empty the condensation tray and clean the lint screen on both sides
  • Verify the circuit breaker for your Bosch oven — wall ovens require a dedicated 40-amp circuit and may trip under self-clean loads
  • Note any blinking error code on the display before calling — Bosch codes map to specific components and help us prepare the right parts

FAQ

Why won't my Bosch dishwasher dry dishes like my old one did?

Bosch dishwashers use condensation drying, not a heated fan. Hot dishes transfer moisture to the cool stainless walls, where it drains away. If dishes come out wet, the rinse-aid dispenser may be empty, the final rinse temperature may be low, or the condensation vent is blocked. We diagnose all three during the visit.

How much does a typical Bosch appliance repair cost?

Most Bosch repairs range from $180 to $450 depending on the appliance and part needed. The $60 diagnostic fee is credited toward the total. We provide a written estimate before starting any work.

My Bosch washer shakes violently during spin. Is that fixable?

Yes. Bosch front-loaders develop spin vibration when the shock absorbers or suspension springs wear out, typically after 5 to 7 years of regular use. We replace these on-site in about 45 minutes.

Is it worth repairing a Bosch dishwasher or should I replace it?

Bosch dishwashers are built to last 10 to 13 years and cost $800 to $2,200 to replace. If your unit is under 8 years old, a $200 to $400 repair is almost always the better financial decision. We give you an honest assessment.

Can you fix Bosch heat-pump dryers? They seem uncommon here.

Yes. Heat-pump dryers are less common in the US than in Europe, but they are growing fast in the Bay Area due to energy codes and ventless installation requirements. We service them regularly and understand the sealed refrigerant loop, condenser cleaning, and thermistor diagnostics.
