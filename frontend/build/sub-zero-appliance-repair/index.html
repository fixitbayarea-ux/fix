Common Problems We Fix

  • Sub-Zero running warm — condenser coils behind the kick plate packed with dust and pet hair, reducing heat dissipation below operating threshold
  • Sub-Zero freezer column building ice on the back wall — defrost heater has failed or defrost timer relay is stuck in cooling mode
  • Sub-Zero compressor cycling on and off every few minutes — low refrigerant charge from a micro-leak at a brazed joint in the sealed system
  • Sub-Zero wine cooler not maintaining set temperature — thermostat or thermistor has drifted out of calibration after 8 or more years of use
  • Sub-Zero door not sealing properly — magnetic gasket has weakened and no longer pulls flush against the cabinet frame
  • Sub-Zero control panel beeping and flashing error codes — faulty temperature sensor or a relay on the main control board has failed
  • Sub-Zero built-in louder than normal — condenser fan motor bearing is wearing out and vibrating against the housing
  • Sub-Zero undercounter ice maker leaking water — cracked water inlet fitting behind the unit where the supply line connects

What to Check Before You Call

  • Check the condenser coils under your Sub-Zero — they need cleaning every 6 to 12 months, especially in homes with pets or heavy kitchen use
  • Test the door seal by placing a dollar bill in the gasket and closing the door — if it slides out easily, the magnetic seal is worn
  • Check if the temperature display shows an error code or flashing numbers — note these for the technician before calling
  • Confirm the unit has at least a quarter-inch of clearance from surrounding cabinetry for proper air circulation
  • If the compressor is running constantly, temporarily lower the temperature setting to reduce load while waiting for service

FAQ

Is it worth repairing a Sub-Zero or should I replace it?

Almost always worth repairing. Sub-Zeros are built to last 20 or more years and cost $8,000 to $20,000 to replace. A $400 to $800 repair on a unit that still has 5 to 10 years of life remaining is a straightforward financial decision.

How often do Sub-Zero condenser coils need cleaning?

Every 6 to 12 months. This is the single most important maintenance task for any Sub-Zero. Dust and pet hair on the condenser coils are responsible for the majority of Sub-Zero overheating calls we receive.

Can you service a Sub-Zero that is built into custom cabinetry?

Yes. We regularly service built-in Sub-Zeros in Pacific Heights, Noe Valley, and Peninsula homes. We know how to pull the unit from custom cabinetry without damaging the surrounding millwork or panel overlays.

My Sub-Zero compressor makes a clicking sound. Is that serious?

Clicking usually indicates the compressor is attempting to start but failing — either from a bad start relay, overheating due to dirty condenser coils, or a more serious sealed-system issue. We diagnose the specific root cause during the service visit.

Do you also service Wolf cooking products?

Yes. Sub-Zero and Wolf are manufactured by the same company. Many Bay Area kitchens have both brands. We service Sub-Zero refrigeration and Wolf cooking products and can diagnose multiple units during the same visit.
